The **Messages** module gives your team a dedicated space for internal communication that is completely separate from customer-facing support tickets. Think of it as your store's built-in team chat — where you can discuss orders in progress, coordinate on vendor issues, and keep all relevant context in one place without cluttering the customer-visible ticket timeline. Access it by clicking **Messages** in the sidebar.

## What Messages is for

Messages is designed for **internal collaboration** — not customer communication. While ticket comments are part of the customer support record and may be visible to the customer, Messages threads are team-only by default. Use Messages when you need to:

- Discuss how to handle a complex ticket before responding to the customer
- Coordinate with a vendor about a purchase order without creating a formal ticket
- Share context about an order with another team member who is picking up the case
- Run an ongoing discussion thread about a specific product, PO, or vendor relationship

## Linking entities to threads

One of the most powerful features of Messages is the ability to link a platform entity — such as an order, ticket, vendor, purchase order, or customer — directly into a thread. Entity links appear as interactive references inside the message, and clicking one opens the full detail page for that entity in a single click.

This means when your team discusses "Order #10452" or "Vendor: AceKeys Ltd", the relevant context is one click away without anyone needing to search or navigate manually.

Supported linkable entities include:

- **Orders** — link directly to the order detail view
- **Tickets** — reference an open or resolved support case
- **Vendors** — link to the vendor detail and history
- **Purchase Orders (POs)** — connect the conversation to a specific procurement cycle
- **Customers** — reference a customer profile for context

## Role badges and visibility

Each message is displayed with a colour-coded **role badge** showing whether the sender is an Admin, Support team member, or Vendor. This makes it immediately clear who is contributing to a thread and from what context — useful in busy channels where multiple roles participate.

<Info>
  **Vendors see a constrained version of Messages.** They can only read and participate in threads they have been explicitly included in. Vendor participants cannot see general team channels or threads they were not invited to.
</Info>

## Mobile experience

On mobile devices, Messages collapses to a single-pane experience optimised for chat on the go. The channel/DM list and the active thread display as separate full-screen views that you navigate between — giving you the full messaging capability without a cluttered layout on a small screen.

## How Messages differs from ticket comments

| | Messages | Ticket comments |
|-|----------|-----------------|
| **Audience** | Internal team (and invited vendors) | Customer and team (customer-facing) |
| **Purpose** | Team collaboration and coordination | Customer support record and communication |
| **Entity linking** | Orders, tickets, vendors, POs, customers | Linked to the ticket itself |
| **Visibility** | Team-only by default | Part of the customer-facing support case |

<Tip>
  Use the ticket's **internal notes** feature for commentary that should live inside the ticket record but stay hidden from the customer. Use Messages for broader team discussions where the conversation doesn't need to be attached to a specific ticket.
</Tip>

## Starting a new thread

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Messages">
    Click **Messages** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a channel or start a DM">
    Click the **+** icon next to Channels to create a new channel, or click **New Message** to start a direct message with a specific team member.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link an entity (optional)">
    In the message composer, use the entity link button to attach a related order, ticket, vendor, PO, or customer to your message.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write and send">
    Type your message and press **Enter** or click **Send**. The thread is now visible to all channel members or your DM recipient.
  </Step>
</Steps>
