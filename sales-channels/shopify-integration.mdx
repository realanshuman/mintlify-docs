The Shopify integration lets you sell digital goods directly from your own Shopify storefront while Tokensupply handles key reservation, fulfilment, and delivery behind the scenes. When a paid order arrives, a key is reserved from your inventory, the line item is fulfilled in Shopify, and the customer receives a tokenised link to reveal their key — all without any manual steps.

## Connect your Shopify store

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Shopify connector">
    In the Tokensupply sidebar, navigate to **Integrations → Shopify**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your shop domain">
    Type your Shopify shop domain in the **Shop Domain** field. Use the format `your-store.myshopify.com`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your Admin API access token">
    Paste your Shopify **Admin API access token** into the token field. Tokensupply uses this token to read orders, fulfil line items, and list your products.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and validate">
    Click **Save**. Tokensupply validates the credentials, lists your Shopify products, and displays the connection status. If the connection succeeds, the status indicator turns active and your Shopify products become available for mapping.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Generate a dedicated custom app in your Shopify Admin (**Settings → Apps and sales channels → Develop apps**) and scope the token to the minimum permissions required: `read_products`, `write_orders`, and `write_fulfillments`.
</Note>

## Map products to Shopify variants

After connecting, open any product in Tokensupply and go to its **Sales Channels** tab. From there you can map the product to a specific Shopify variant and configure per-mapping settings.

Each mapping exposes two controls:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Status" icon="toggle-on">
    Set the mapping to **Active** to enable order processing for this product, or **Paused** to suspend delivery without removing the mapping entirely.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Auto Deliver" icon="bolt">
    When enabled, Tokensupply automatically reserves and delivers a key as soon as a paid Shopify order is received — no manual intervention needed.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Manage mappings in bulk

To review or update all Shopify mappings at once, navigate to **Sales Channel → Shopify**. From this view you can filter by status, toggle Active and Auto Deliver across multiple products, and audit every product–variant relationship without opening individual product pages.

## Order flow

Once a product mapping is active, the following sequence happens automatically for every paid Shopify order:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Order received">
    Tokensupply detects the paid order from Shopify.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Key reserved">
    An available key for the mapped product is reserved from your inventory, preventing it from being sold elsewhere.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Line item fulfilled">
    Tokensupply marks the Shopify order line item as fulfilled via the Admin API.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Delivery link emailed">
    The customer receives an email containing a tokenised delivery link to your branded customer portal, where they can reveal their key.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for a smooth setup

<Accordion title="Scope your Admin API token carefully">
  Only grant the permissions Tokensupply needs. The minimum required scopes are `read_products`, `write_orders`, and `write_fulfillments`. Overly broad tokens are a security risk and may be flagged by Shopify's app review.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Only enable Auto Deliver for products with healthy stock">
  If Auto Deliver is on and your inventory runs out, incoming orders will not be fulfilled automatically. Monitor your stock levels or set reorder thresholds before activating Auto Deliver on high-volume products.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Cancellations release reserved keys">
  If a Shopify order is cancelled upstream, any key that was reserved for that order is released back into your available inventory automatically. You do not need to manually reconcile stock after a cancellation.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Use the **Sales Channel → Shopify** bulk view to do a pre-launch audit — confirm every mapped product has Active status, healthy stock, and the correct variant selected before you open your store to traffic.
</Tip>
