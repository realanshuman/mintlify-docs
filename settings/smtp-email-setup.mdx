By default, TokenSupply sends transactional emails through Resend. Connecting your own SMTP server lets you send deliveries, notifications, and vendor invites from a domain you control — giving recipients a consistent, branded sender address and keeping your emails out of spam. If your SMTP connection ever fails, TokenSupply automatically falls back to Resend so critical emails are never blocked.

## Configuring SMTP

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Email Setup">
    In the sidebar, click **Settings**, then select the **Email Setup** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your server details">
    Fill in the following fields:

    | Field | What to enter |
    |-------|---------------|
    | **Host** | Your SMTP server address (e.g. `smtp.gmail.com`, `smtp.sendgrid.net`) |
    | **Port** | Typically `587` for TLS or `465` for SSL |
    | **Username** | Your SMTP account username (often your full email address) |
    | **Password** | Your SMTP password or app-specific password |
    | **From Email** | The address your emails will appear to come from |
    | **From Name** | The display name customers and vendors will see |
    | **Encryption** | Choose **TLS**, **SSL**, or **None** |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your encryption">
    Select **TLS** unless your provider specifically requires SSL or no encryption. TLS on port 587 is the most reliable and secure default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a test email">
    Click **Send Test Email**. TokenSupply sends a test message to your registered account email. Check your inbox — and your spam folder — to confirm delivery and inspect the sender address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your settings">
    Once the test succeeds, click **Save**. All subsequent transactional emails will route through your SMTP server.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Encryption options

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="TLS (Recommended)">
    Use TLS on port **587**. This is the most widely supported and secure option and works with virtually all modern SMTP providers including Gmail, Outlook, and SendGrid.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="SSL">
    Use SSL on port **465** if your provider specifically requires it. Older than TLS but still secure — check your provider's documentation to confirm.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="None">
    Only use **None** in local development or testing environments. Sending production emails without encryption exposes credentials and message content in transit.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Resend fallback

<Info>
  If your SMTP server is unreachable or returns an error, TokenSupply automatically routes critical transactional emails — including vendor invites, order deliveries, and system notifications — through Resend. This ensures customers and vendors are never blocked by a temporary mail server issue. The fallback is silent; no manual intervention is required.
</Info>

## Provider-specific tips

<Accordion title="Gmail and Google Workspace">
  Google requires an **app-specific password** when 2-Step Verification is enabled on your account — your regular Google password will not work. Generate one at [myaccount.google.com/apppasswords](https://myaccount.google.com/apppasswords) and use it in the **Password** field. Use `smtp.gmail.com` as the host and port `587` with TLS.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Outlook / Microsoft 365">
  Use `smtp.office365.com` as the host, port `587`, and TLS. You may need to enable SMTP AUTH for the sending mailbox in the Microsoft 365 admin centre — it is disabled by default on some tenants.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="SendGrid">
  Use `smtp.sendgrid.net`, port `587`, TLS. Your username is the literal string `apikey` and your password is your SendGrid API key with the **Mail Send** permission enabled.
</Accordion>

## Best practices

<Tip>
  Always click **Send Test Email** after changing any SMTP credential — even a minor update like a password rotation. A successful test confirms the entire send path is working before a real transactional email needs to go out.
</Tip>

- Use a dedicated sending address (e.g. `orders@yourdomain.com`) rather than a personal inbox to keep transactional mail cleanly separated.
- Rotate app-specific passwords if you suspect a credential has been compromised — update the Password field and re-test immediately.
- If emails are landing in spam, check that your domain has SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records configured at your DNS provider.
